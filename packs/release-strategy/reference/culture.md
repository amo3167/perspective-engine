# Sportsbet Organizational Culture

Reference document for meeting agents. Understanding the company culture is essential for calibrating advice on how to introduce internal innovation work — what will land well, what will trigger concern, and which cultural values to leverage.

---

## Core Values

Sportsbet operates under an **"Our Fun, Their Fun"** philosophy that balances high-energy delivery with employee wellbeing. Three internal mantras shape daily work:

- **Play 3D**: Think beyond your own team. Consider how decisions impact adjacent teams and the broader business. This is the primary bridge between technical output and organizational impact.
- **Got Your Back**: Psychological safety is an explicit value. Taking risks is encouraged, with a **Reflect, Learn, Improve (RLI)** framework for when experiments don't work out.
- **Green Tick Feeling**: Success is personalized — launching a major platform feature counts, but so does making school pickup on time. Work-life integration is a stated priority.

## Engineering & Product Culture

The tech organization generally rates its culture higher than company-wide averages:

- **Hackathons and Hack Days** are a regular fixture, giving engineers dedicated time to experiment outside BAU delivery. This is a strong cultural precedent for innovation work like multi-agent simulation.
- **"License to Drive"** culture expects engineers to be decisive and take ownership of their stack. Autonomy is valued over permission-seeking.
- **Social environment**: The Melbourne office features communal spaces (snooker, tennis), regular "Showtime" events, and Friday socials. Informal sharing is a natural part of how work gets visibility.

## Employee Sentiment

Recent sentiment data (2024–2026) shows a consistent pattern:

### Strengths
- **Generous benefits**: 5 weeks annual leave, extensive parental leave (including foster/IVF), competitive bonuses
- **Caliber of peers**: Consistently praised for "smart, down-to-earth" colleagues in technical roles
- **Flexibility**: High marks for remote work options and output-focused (not clock-watching) culture

### Growth Areas
- **Pace**: Frequently described as "not a place for those who want to cruise" — high-pressure and fast-moving
- **Inclusion**: Some feedback around male-dominated atmosphere, with internal groups like *Women in Tech* actively working to address this
- **Management layers**: Occasional disconnect between upper management and direct reports, with some "manage up" dynamics

## Implications for Releasing Innovation Work

These cultural signals matter for the release-strategy decision:

| Cultural Factor | Opportunity | Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Got Your Back / RLI** | Strong cover for experimental work — "we tried something, here's what we learned" is an accepted narrative | Only works if the framing is genuinely exploratory, not a polished product pitch |
| **Hackathon culture** | Multi-agent simulation fits naturally into the hackathon/innovation tradition — it's an engineering exploration | If positioned as "production AI," it exits the safe hackathon frame and enters the accountability frame |
| **License to Drive** | Engineers expect autonomy — sharing tools organically is normal behavior, not a governance event | Autonomy culture means you can share quietly, but it also means others will form opinions without guidance |
| **Smart peers** | High-caliber technical colleagues will appreciate the engineering challenge and give constructive feedback | They'll also immediately see the implications and ask hard questions — be ready for them |
| **Fast pace / high pressure** | People value tools that help them think faster, not tools that add process | Anything that looks like "more meetings about meetings" will be dismissed instantly |
| **Play 3D** | The strongest framing lever — this work demonstrates cross-domain thinking and multi-perspective analysis | Play 3D is also used to challenge work that's too self-contained — "how does this help the business?" |
